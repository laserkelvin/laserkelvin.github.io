Hi!

My name is Kelvin Lee, and I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian where my research interests lie at the intersection of Chemistry, Astronomy, and Data Science. I work closely with spectroscopists, chemists, and astronomers to solve a whole suite of problems ranging from astrochemistry and astrophysics of dying stars, to more down-to-earth problems like molecular structure and reactivity, complex mixture analysis. What I bring to these cookouts is my ability to understand context from all of the diverse fields, and produce practical solutions using domain-specific knowledge and general data science skills such as data cleaning and machine learning.

What drives me

  • My most recent interests lie with taking high-level ideas - for example, performing inference on spectroscopic data - and implementing low-level solutions using fast numerical Python and Julia.
  • I'm also passionate about taking unstructured data, and creating beautiful (at least my opinion of beautiful!) visualizations that are informative on multiple levels; I'm a big admirer of Tufte style visualizations and teaching. On occasion I like to dabble with creating graphics with Blender.
  • I think reproducibility in the sciences is one we need to address as soon as possible; experiments should be transparent and reproducible from the ground up, and there is simply no excuse for not doing so in the age of computing!
  • Innovation; there's always a better way to do whatever it is you're doing. The trick, however, is knowing when it's good enough to stop!
